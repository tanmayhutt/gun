The Official 6-Player High Stakes Card Game

Players & Setup

  • Take a standard 52-card deck and remove all four 2s, leaving a 48-card deck.
  • The game is played by 6 players divided into 2 teams (3 players per team).
  • Team Selection: Cards are dealt anti-clockwise. The first three players to receive a Jack form Team A, and the remaining three form Team B.
  • Players sit in a circle in an alternating order, ensuring that every player is seated between two members of the opposing team.
  • First Dealer: The deck is shuffled again and dealt anti-clockwise. The first player to receive a Jack becomes the first dealer.
  • Game Flow: All dealing and gameplay strictly proceeds in an anti-clockwise direction.
  • The card hierarchy follows standard rules (Ace is highest, down to 3).

Dealing & "The Gun"

  • The 48 cards are shuffled thoroughly. Initially, each player is dealt a batch of exactly 4 cards.
  • A player from the team opposite the dealer gets to choose the Trump by selecting one card from their hand and keeping it face down (blind).
  • The "Gun" Rule: If any player feels their hand is exceptionally strong, they can declare "Gun". They must sequentially open their cards to attempt to win the entire round.
  • If no one declares Gun, the remaining 24 cards are dealt out. Each player now has 8 cards.

The Play

  • The person who chose the blind Trump card starts the game by playing their first card.
  • Play continues sequentially around the table. Everyone must follow the suit led if they have it (similar to standard trick-taking conventions).

  • Since there are 8 cards per player, there will be exactly 8 Hands (Tricks) played in a round.
  • Revealing the Trump: The Trump card remains blind until a player is unable to follow the suit that was led. At that point, they ask the player who chose the Trump to reveal it. Once flipped, that suit officially becomes Trump.

Scoring & Penalties

Points are considered penalties. Teams subtract points upon victory and accumulate points upon failure. Penalties are assigned directly to the current dealer.

Trump Team

Target: 5 Hands

Failure: +10 pts

Success: -10 pts

Defending Team

Target: 4 Hands

Failure: +5 pts

Success: -5 pts

Gun (Trump Team)

Target: All Hands

Failure: +36 pts

Success: -36 pts

Gun (Defending Team)

Target: All Hands

Failure: +18 pts

Success: -18 pts

The Dealer & "Getting Sold"

  • When a team reaches exactly 0 points, the dealership moves to a member of the opposing team.
  • If a dealer accumulates exactly 48 points, they are declared "Sold". The sold dealer must perform a penalty dare assigned by the rest of the players.

Origin of the Game

The game of Gun originated in Bhati Village in Madh Island, Mumbai, India. It spread to the rest of the world from the home of Imraan Rashid aka Mahatma.
